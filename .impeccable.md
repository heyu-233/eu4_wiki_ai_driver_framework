## Design Context

### Users
EU4 players who use a large local wiki mirror while planning campaigns, achievements, mission routes, events, modifiers, traits, and console/debug lookups. They need quick answers with citations, not a marketing page.

### Brand Personality
Calm, archival, strategic. The interface should feel like a compact strategy desk: warm enough to invite long reading, precise enough for repeated querying, and restrained enough that citations and retrieved evidence stay central.

### Aesthetic Direction
Reference: rightclicklogo.com for its sand/cream canvas, olive accent, compact builder-like panel, rounded but quiet controls, and generous breathing room. Apply that feeling to a local EU4 knowledge workbench, with subtle map/archive cues and no decorative overload.

### Design Principles
- Keep the first screen as the usable tool: question input, answer, citations, and retrieval are immediately visible.
- Favor warm paper surfaces, olive ink, thin borders, and low-contrast panels over bright SaaS blue.
- Use compact controls and dense source lists because the user is actively researching, not browsing a landing page.
- Make evidence legible: citations, retrieved snippets, status, and AI answer should each have a distinct visual role.
- Avoid stale decorative motifs; any historical flavor must support reading and orientation.
